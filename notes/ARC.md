# ARC.md

This novella is structured as a slow, irreversible narrowing.

It begins after something important has already been said.
It ends after its consequences can no longer be undone.

## Act I – The Aftermath

The story opens with the pilot Alex reflecting on a truth he has already spoken.

We do not know yet:
- what he said
- when he said it
- or how she responded

We see:
- the star
- the platform
- the daily routines
- the quiet between them

The stellar physicist Alex remains kind.
She remains warm.
She does not change her behavior.

That is what hurts.

## Act II – Before the Truth

The narrative moves backward and sideways.

We see:
- how the two Alexes met
- how their working relationship became personal
- how warmth and reliance blurred into attachment

The pilot Alex begins to want more than he is being given.

The physicist Alex remains open but emotionally unclaimed.

The imbalance grows.

## Act III – The Moment

We finally see the scene where the pilot Alex speaks.

It is not dramatic.
It is not loud.

It is a quiet confession of need.

The physicist Alex listens.
She does not reject him.
She does not promise him.

She simply does not change.

This is the point of no return.

## Act IV – The Present

We return to the present.

The platform continues to function.
The star continues to burn.

The two Alexes must now live inside what has been revealed.

Everything is the same.
Nothing is.

## Act V – The Choice

The pilot Alex must decide:
- whether to stay
- or whether to emotionally withdraw to survive

There is no external escape.

Only an internal one.

The story ends with him choosing how he will remain.

Not hopeful.
Not tragic.

Just honest.
