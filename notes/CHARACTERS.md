# CHARACTERS.md

There are two central characters.

They are both named **Alex**.

Their shared name is intentional.
It reflects their similarity, their mirroring, and the way they could have been each other in another life.

They are not interchangeable.

## Alex (Stellar Physicist)

Alex is a woman.

She has long red hair that catches the light from the star and makes her visible even in shadow.

She is responsible for understanding the star.

She models:
- flare probability
- mass loss
- magnetic instability
- long-term stellar evolution

She experiences the star as data first, sensation second.

She is:
- kind
- patient
- emotionally contained
- capable of caring deeply without attaching her identity to being loved back

She can admire something vast without needing it to belong to her.

Alex does not withhold affection.
She simply does not organize her life around it.

She is warm, but self-sufficient.

## Alex (Pilot)

Alex is a man.

He is responsible for keeping the platform alive.

He maintains:
- orientation
- vector alignment
- cavity stability
- emergency response during stellar surges

He experiences the star as force first, abstraction second.

He is:
- attentive
- emotionally open
- deeply affected by proximity
- and shaped by the need to be needed

He lives by movement and trajectory, but is trapped in stillness.

He feels the star.
He feels her.

He does not need ownership.
He needs **reciprocity.**

## The Relationship

The two Alexes:
- work together
- rely on each other
- share meals, silences, and fear

They are close.

They are not equal in how they love.

She can exist in warmth without wanting more.
He cannot.

This imbalance is not spoken aloud at first.

It grows in the space between what is given and what is hoped for.

No one is lying.
No one is pretending.

They simply want different things.
