# MOTIVATION.md

This novel exists because some forms of pain cannot be solved, only witnessed.

**The Two Alexes** is being written to explore a specific human experience:
Loving someone who is kind, warm, and emotionally present, but who does not need you in the same way you need them.

This is not a story about rejection.
It is a story about asymmetry.

It is about what happens when:
- care exists without commitment
- warmth exists without belonging
- and proximity exists without reciprocity

The author has lived inside this emotional geometry.
This book is an attempt to give that experience a shape that can be looked at instead of hidden from.

## Why this story

Most stories about love are about mutuality.
Most stories about heartbreak are about cruelty.

Very few stories are about **kindness that still hurts**.

This book is meant to live in that uncomfortable space.

Two people can do nothing wrong and still break each other.

That truth deserves to exist on the page.

## Why now

This novel comes after *The Long Wake*.

That book was about being alone inside a system that could not care.

This book is about being with someone who does care, but not in the way you need.

It is a progression.
From isolation.
To proximity.
To the risk of wanting.

## What success looks like

This book is successful if:
- readers recognize themselves in the asymmetry
- the story feels quiet, heavy, and honest
- no one is villainized for not loving correctly

The goal is not catharsis.
The goal is recognition.

This is a book for people who have been warmed by someone who could not stay.

That is why it is being written.
