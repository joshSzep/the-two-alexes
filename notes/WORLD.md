# WORLD.md

## Overview

**The Two Alexes** takes place on a human-built stellar stabilization platform embedded inside the corona of a natural, unstable star.

The star is being captured, shaped, and stabilized to become a long-term energy source and gravitational anchor for a future human system.

The platform exists inside a carved cavity in the star’s outer plasma layers, held open by continuous magnetic and gravitational field manipulation.

Outside the habitat:
- solar plasma
- extreme radiation
- violent magnetic storms

Inside:
- a narrow, artificial pocket of survivable space
- sustained by constant energy expenditure and perfect alignment

There is no planetary surface.
There is no nearby station.
There is no rescue.

## The Star

The star is:
- natural
- massive
- unstable
- prone to violent flares and mass ejections

It was chosen because:
- its energy output is immense
- its instability makes it impossible to leave unattended
- its lifetime can be extended through intervention

If left alone, it will either collapse or blow off too much mass to be useful.

Humanity is trying to keep it alive.

## The Platform

The platform is not anchored to solid matter.
It is suspended inside the star’s corona using:

- magnetic confinement fields that redirect plasma
- gravitational field shaping that stabilizes a hollow cavity
- continuous active correction by onboard systems

The platform must maintain:
- position
- orientation
- field strength
- thermal balance

Any failure results in the cavity collapsing and the platform being consumed by the star.

## The Mission

The mission is long-term and open-ended.

The goal is not to “finish” stabilizing the star.
The goal is to **keep it from dying**.

The platform’s crew must:
- monitor stellar behavior
- adjust containment fields
- redirect mass ejections
- maintain station alignment

The mission duration is measured in decades.

Resupply and replacement are either impossible or so slow as to be meaningless.

## Life on the Platform

The habitat is small.

There are:
- living quarters
- control rooms
- observation decks
- maintenance corridors

There is no crowd.
No society.
No anonymity.

Every movement, every silence, every choice is shared.

The star is always visible.

## Communication

Communication with Earth or other human systems exists but is delayed by extreme distance.

Conversations take years to complete.

Operational decisions cannot wait for outside input.

The crew is alone in practice, even if not technically isolated.

## Failure

If either:
- the star destabilizes beyond control
- or the platform loses alignment

Everyone dies.

The star continues.

The universe does not care.
